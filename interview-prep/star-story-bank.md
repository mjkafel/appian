# STAR Story Bank

## Story 1: Booz Allen Modernization Solutioning

**Use for:** implementation ownership, stakeholder alignment, requirements, delivery.

> One example is my current VA modernization work. The environment has legacy systems, secure data concerns, and multiple stakeholder groups. I own solutioning from requirements through development handoff. That includes facilitating sessions, mapping workflows, documenting integration options, and defining API and data considerations. The result has been 7+ initiatives delivered on time and clearer execution for product and development teams.

## Story 2: ECM-L Transition Plans

**Use for:** process modeling, data mapping, Appian process automation fit.

> A strong example is my correspondence modernization transition planning work. The goal was to move automated letter generation away from legacy tooling and toward a modern enterprise correspondence capability. I documented current and target workflows, mapped required data, defined integration assumptions, and identified roles, permissions, workflow configuration, and downstream lifecycle events. The result was a clearer implementation path that SMEs and engineering teams could validate.

## Story 3: iCIMS Enterprise SaaS Integrations

**Use for:** APIs, integrations, external customers, SaaS delivery.

> At iCIMS, I worked directly in enterprise SaaS implementation. I gathered requirements, wrote API and SFTP specs, configured SAML/OAuth identity integrations, and supported 10+ concurrent customer projects. That experience is relevant because Appian implementations also depend on secure data exchange, system ownership, integration validation, and clear customer communication.

## Story 4: Oracle Observability And Technical Consulting

**Use for:** technical troubleshooting, cloud-native experience, technical credibility.

> At Oracle, I worked in a technically complex environment with Kubernetes-based deployments and observability tooling like Grafana, Prometheus, Jaeger, and Elasticsearch. That gave me a strong operational mindset: understand impact, look at logs and metrics, isolate the failing layer, document what happened, and transfer knowledge to the customer or team.

## Story 5: AI-Assisted Requirements Analysis

**Use for:** AI/RAG interest, prompt engineering, responsible AI.

> In modernization work, I have used AI-assisted analysis to improve documentation and requirements workflows. I use AI to organize notes, identify gaps, draft clearer language, and speed up documentation. I do not treat it as an authority. I validate against SME input, architecture constraints, and source material. That mindset is important for enterprise AI because the goal is useful automation with governance and human accountability.

## Story 6: Architecture Documentation Standardization

**Use for:** documentation, enablement, process improvement, self-sufficiency.

> I led architecture documentation standardization in Confluence and produced 30+ architecture pages across operational, system, service, and data views. The goal was not documentation for its own sake. It was creating a shared source of truth so teams could align faster and make better implementation decisions.
