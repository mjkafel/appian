# Role Map

## Role In One Sentence

Appian is looking for a post-sales Senior Consultant who can work through the full customer implementation lifecycle: define business processes, gather requirements, design data models, build Appian applications, integrate with enterprise systems, apply AI features, test solutions, and enable customers to become self-sufficient.

## What The Posting Is Really Testing

| Posting Signal | What They Are Testing | Your Proof Point |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Full project lifecycle | Can you own discovery through implementation? | Booz requirements-to-handoff ownership across 7+ initiatives |
| Develop software solutions on Appian | Can you get hands-on with a platform? | SQL/Python/API exposure; iCIMS SaaS implementation; Oracle technical consulting |
| Business processes and technical needs | Can you translate both directions? | BPMN workflows, data mapping, stakeholder sessions |
| Relational data models | Can you reason about data structure? | Data dictionary work, data-flow analysis, SQL toolkit |
| APIs and multiple systems | Can you integrate enterprise systems? | iCIMS APIs/SFTP/SAML/OAuth; Booz API contracts and integration assumptions |
| AI/RAG/agentic workflows | Can you think responsibly about AI inside process? | AI-assisted requirements analysis; agentic/RAG architecture awareness |
| Agile team delivery | Can you work in sprints and cross-functional teams? | Product owners, SMEs, architects, development teams, acceptance criteria |
| Mentoring/developing others | Can you help teams become self-sufficient? | Reusable frameworks, documentation standardization, handoff artifacts |
| Customer Success | Can you drive outcomes after sale? | Customer-facing consulting, enterprise integrations, knowledge transfer |

## Strongest Fit Statement

> This role is a strong fit because it is not only technical build and not only advisory. It sits where business process, data modeling, integrations, platform implementation, testing, and customer enablement meet. That is the lane I have been building toward.

## Risk Areas And Best Answers

| Risk | Best Response |
| --- | --- |
| No direct Appian development yet | Be honest. Bridge to process modeling, data, APIs, SQL/Python, platform ramp, and technical consulting. |
| Role asks for hands-on development | Position as technical consultant who can get hands-on, not pure full-stack developer. |
| AI/RAG/agents may be newer | Frame AI experience as AI-assisted analysis plus responsible enterprise AI architecture awareness. |
| OOP/software testing depth | Be honest if not deepest lane; bridge to technical implementation, validation, acceptance criteria, and willingness to ramp. |
| Public sector citizenship note | You are U.S.-based and have Tier 2 MBI Public Trust on resume. |

## Must-Land Ideas

1. I understand Appian Customer Success is post-sales implementation, not just account management.
2. I can define business processes and translate them into technical requirements.
3. I can reason through data models, data ownership, and SQL validation.
4. I have real enterprise integration experience.
5. I am interested in hands-on platform build, not just documentation.
6. I can ramp on Appian through bootcamp, project work, and self-study.
7. I care about customer enablement and self-sufficiency.
