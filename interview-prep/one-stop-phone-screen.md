## 45-Minute Call Flow

Use this path if you do not want to jump around.

1. Start with `[INTRO]`.
2. If they ask why Appian, use `[WHY-APPIAN]`.
3. If they ask why leaving, use `[WHY-LEAVE]`.
4. For fit, use `[FIT]`, then pull one proof point from `[STAR]`.
5. For technical questions, go to `[DATA]`, `[API]`, `[AI]` or `[TECH]`.
6. End with 4 to 6 questions from `[ASK]`.

Most important sentence:

> My strongest value is translating complex business processes into buildable solutions, from workflow and data design through integration, testing and customer enablement.

## Your Goal

Make them believe this:

> Matt can become effective on Appian because he already understands the consulting motion: learn the process. Model the workflow and data. Define integrations. Create implementation-ready direction. Test against acceptance criteria. Enable the customer.

## Recruiter Scorecard

Make these five points obvious:

- You understand this is post-sales implementation, not account management.
- You can talk to business users and technical teams.
- You can reason through process, data, integrations and testing.
- You want to become hands-on with the platform.
- Your Appian gap is product-specific, not a role-fit problem.

## [ASK] Questions To Ask

Ask 4 to 6. Since this is 45 minutes, you may have more time.

1. How hands-on is this Senior Consultant expected to be in the Appian build versus leading design and client delivery?
2. What does success look like in the first 90 days?
3. How quickly do new consultants typically move from bootcamp into client project work?
4. What kinds of applications are customers asking Appian Customer Success to build most often right now?
5. How much of the current work involves AI use cases like RAG, agentic workflows, or AI-assisted process automation?
6. What technical skills tend to separate strong consultants from average ones on this team?
7. How does Appian measure whether a customer has become self-sufficient after implementation?
8. What is the team culture like around code review, pair programming, and mentoring?
9. For someone with my background, where would you expect the steepest ramp to be?
10. Based on our conversation, do you have any concerns about my fit that I can clarify?
